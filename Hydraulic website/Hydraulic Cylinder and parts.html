Hydraulic Cylinders

Hydraulic cylinders are fundamental components of hydraulic systems, engineered to convert hydraulic oil pressure into linear motion. These actuators are vital for a wide range of industrial applications, enabling precise and powerful movement in various machinery.

A hydraulic cylinder consists of four main components: -

1. Cylinder Barrel: - This barrel houses the piston and piston rod, and is designed to withstand high internal pressures.

2. Piston: - Fitted with seals to prevent fluid leakage, the piston is connected to the piston rod and transmits hydraulic pressure into linear force.

3. Piston Rod: - Typically made of stainless steel to transfer the linear force to the external load

4. Seals: - Prevent leakage of pressurized oil and ensure efficient operation.

There are various types of hydraulic cylinders including Single-Acting Cylinders, Double-Acting Cylinders, and Telescopic Cylinders.
